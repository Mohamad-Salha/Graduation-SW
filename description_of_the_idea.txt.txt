1. Overview

This system manages a driving school, including students, teachers, trainers, and administrators. It supports course enrollment, theoretical and practical training, scheduling, payments, exam management, and notifications.

The system is designed to be scalable, maintainable, and user-friendly, with clear roles and responsibilities.

2. Users and Roles
2.1 Student

Sign Up / Sign In

Students can sign up by filling in required information.

A request is sent to the admin for approval.

After approval, the student can sign in.

Course Enrollment

Upon first login, a list of available courses (licenses) appears.

Students choose a course before proceeding.

Only one active course per student is allowed at a time.

Teacher Assignment

Students choose a theoretical teacher for traffic signs, engine info, and road knowledge.

Schedule Management

Theoretical Schedule: Includes lecture times, teacher, date, location, and other details.

Practical Schedule: Becomes available after passing the theoretical exam.

Students can book practical sessions based on trainer availability.

Session limits are enforced to ensure fairness.

Exams

After completing the minimum number of practical sessions and paying all dues, a trainer can mark the student ready for the practical test.

Admin schedules the test (date, time, location) and notifies the student.

Students who fail can request a retest.

2.2 Teacher

Sign In Only

Teachers are added by the admin.

Responsibilities

View assigned students and their lecture progress.

Manage theoretical lecture schedules (static for now).

Mark students as ready for theoretical exams.

Optional quizzes for knowledge confirmation.

Notify admin when students are ready for theoretical exams.

2.3 Trainer

Sign In

Trainers are added by the admin.

Responsibilities

Edit their weekly schedule (availability).

Students book practical sessions based on trainer availability.

Record student attendance and payments for each session.

Track student progress (progress bar / readiness for practical test).

Once conditions are met (minimum sessions + full payment), mark students ready for practical tests.

Admin schedules the practical exam and notifies students.

Filter students by session date for easy payment entry.

2.4 Admin

Dashboard

Full visibility and management of the system: students, teachers, trainers, vehicles, exams, and payments.

Responsibilities

Approve student sign-ups.

Add/list teachers and trainers, view schedules.

Add/list vehicles and assign them to trainers.

Schedule exams (theoretical and practical) and notify students.

Track student exam history and results.

3. Key Features

Role-Based Access: Students, teachers, trainers, and admin have specific dashboards and privileges.

Courses/Licenses: Students choose from available licenses; each license has a minimum number of practical sessions and a price.

Scheduling: Theoretical and practical schedules are managed, with practical sessions linked to trainer availability.

Progress Tracking: Trainers track student progress for practical readiness.

Payments: Trainers record payments for practical sessions.

Exams and Exam Attempts: Each exam attempt is tracked separately to allow multiple retests.

Notifications: System notifies students about exam results, schedules, and retests.

Single Active Course Rule: Students cannot take a new course while an existing course is active.

4. Database Collections (for clarity)
Collection	Purpose
users	Stores admin, teachers, trainers, and students (role-based). Passwords hashed.
licenses	Courses/licenses available for students.
vehicles	Vehicles assigned to trainers.
theoSchedules	Theoretical lecture schedules (teacher, date, location).
practicalSchedules	Practical session schedules (trainer, student, vehicle, date).
studentSessions	Tracks individual practical sessions, payments, and progress.
exams	Scheduled exams (type, date, location, students).
examAttempts	Tracks each attempt per student per exam (allows multiple attempts).
notifications	Stores messages sent to students, trainers, teachers, or admin.
5. Security and Authentication

Password Hashing: All passwords are stored securely using hashing (bcrypt).

Tokens / Sessions: JWT or similar tokens can be used for authentication.

Admin Seed: The first admin is created via a seeding script with a hashed password.

6. Workflow Summary

Student signs up → waits for admin approval.

Student selects a course → chooses a theoretical teacher.

Student attends lectures → optionally takes quizzes → marked ready for theoretical exam.

Student passes theoretical exam → can book practical sessions.

Trainer manages practical sessions, attendance, payments, and progress.

Trainer marks student ready for practical exam → admin schedules exam.

Student passes → receives license → may choose another course.

Failed exams → student requests retest → process repeats.
